Why Your Startup Needs an Edge Firewall in 2026

Alex Hacker

March 15, 2026 • 5 min read

In today's threat landscape, relying solely on backend validation is a recipe for disaster. Attackers use automated scripts to find vulnerabilities like SQL Injection and XSS faster than you can patch them.

An Edge WAF (Web Application Firewall) acts as an invisible shield. It sits directly between the attacker and your server, inspecting payloads and dropping malicious traffic before it ever touches your database.

Without one, even a simple comment box can become an entry point for a devastating database breach.
